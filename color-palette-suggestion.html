Why These Colors?

Primary Blue (#0078a8)

The United Utilities logo features a strong, mid-tone blue that evokes water, the company's core product. Blue also conveys trust, reliability, and professionalism — essential for a utility provider that customers depend on every day.

  • Directly extracted from the site's most-used brand token.
  • High recognition value — users will immediately associate it with the brand.
  • Works well for buttons, links, and interactive elements (sufficient contrast on white).

Secondary Teal (#024e43)

A deep teal/forest-green that appears throughout the site's icons (e.g., "forest-green" water-glass icon). It reinforces themes of nature, sustainability, and environmental responsibility — key messages for a water company.

  • Complements the blue without competing for attention.
  • Ideal for eco/sustainability sections, secondary highlights, and callouts.
  • Pairs well with white text for high-contrast badges.

Accent Magenta (#d00068)

A vivid magenta used sparingly on the live site for CTAs and attention-grabbing elements. It adds energy and urgency without clashing with the calmer blue-teal foundation.

  • High contrast against both blue and teal — perfect for "Pay Now" or alert buttons.
  • Used sparingly so it doesn't dilute brand recognition.
  • Warm hue balances the cool primary palette.

Neutral Text (#1e2a36)

A dark blue-grey rather than pure black. This softens the page visually, reduces eye strain, and subtly ties the neutrals back to the blue brand family.

  • Exceeds WCAG AA contrast on light backgrounds (≥ 4.5:1).
  • Feels more modern and approachable than stark #000000.

Surface & Border (#f5f7f9, #dce1e6)

A slightly cool off-white surface and a gentle blue-tinged border keeps the design feeling fresh and cohesive with the primary blue.

  • Cool undertones tie back to the "water" theme.
  • Prevents the stark look of pure grey neutrals.

Semantic Colors

Success (green), Warning (amber), and Error (red) are chosen for universal recognition and WCAG contrast compliance. They don't appear in the logo but are essential for any UI.
